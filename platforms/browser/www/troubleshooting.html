I. Definition of a good pelletizing result.

In case no granulometric specification is established, a good pelletizing result can be defined as a long period of production without any shutdown due to the cutting system.

If a granulometric specification exists, a good pelletizing result is defined as the production of pellets in accordance to this specification (limited amount of discrepant pellets, respect of defined pellet shape and size...).

For example, a very well known pellet uniformity test is called the “delta weight” method and is performed as follows:

Number of pellets in 4 g x [(wt of 10 largest - wt of 10 smallest)] x 10
8

10-25 % is excellent
26-40% is typical for PP
41-60% is poor

Of course the measurement must be made with a precision balance (0.1 mg).
Note: the pellet main size cannot be controlled if it varies of more than 60% throughout the production (which is the case when the die plate is plugged by frozen polymer).

In the case of a die plate plugged by polymer, it is useful to be able to calculate the number of frozen holes with the following formula:

# holes open = 1672000 x rate in t/h²
(g / 100 pellets) x ( number of knives) x (Cutter RPM)

A typical calculation could be:

[1672000 x 30] / [(2.6) x (30) x (550)] = 1169 holes open.


Knowing the original number of holes in the die plate (1263 for instance), the % of open holes would then be = (1169/1263) x 100 = 92.5%

II. Cavitation

Cavitation occurs when the PCW pressure or flow is too low in the water bowl, or when the knife rotation speed is too high.
The effect of cavitation can be very severe, as it attacks the metal by the creation of small vacuum areas in which the chemical reactions are very violent; this makes the cavitation damage look like very much as corrosion pitting.
This can be avoided by the modification of the PCW circuit setup, the addition of a restriction orifice at the top of the water bowl exit, of the wise choice of a knife model.
Refer to the attached PPT, at the end of this manual, for calculations and guideline for knife model choice.
